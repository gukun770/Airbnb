Airbnb Market Analysis - Part I¶

The idea of this notebook is to give you some insights of airbnb San Francisco market as well as the factors that can affect a host's revenue. Part I will mainly focus on the price part.

monthly income of a host = price * monthly occupancy

Inside Airbnb dataset include:

  • listings.csv
  • calendar.csv
  • reviews.csv

listings.csv consists of details of all the listings including their price, accomodates, ratings, number of reviews, summary, name, owner name, Description, host Id and many other columns decribing details of listings.

calendar.csv consists of details of listings and its availability and its price.

reviews.csv consits of reveiws for each listing.

So the analysis gives us data points that the prices of listings on Airbnb depends upon the room type, property type, number of bedrooms and neighbourhood.It can be seen that the property with type as House (Townhouse included) and the listing as with type as entire house with maximum number of bedooms has highest price. Although it depends upon neighbourhood as well.

As for my webapp, new users do not necessarily know how to set the short term rate, what price per day is considered as 'fair price'. To solve this problem, I used a Knn(n=5) model to take the price from the market (2018-7-5).

  • First, based on users' address, I sent a request to Googlemap about the latitude and longitude of the address as well as the zip-code.
  • Second, based on users' number of bedroom, room_type(entire home/private room/shared room), property_type(house, apartment, others) and zipcode, I can quickly narrow down the search zone with a smaller dataframe. Then I used the knn model to find the price of geographically the 5 closest neighbors using lat and lon.
  • Third, I average my neighbours' price, which is my guideline price to predict the occupancy for the next stage.(see part 2)
